# PDR-091: Precedence Is Not Approval

**Status**: Accepted
**Date**: 2026-06-08
**Adopted**: 2026-06-08
**Related**:
[PDR-089](PDR-089-conservation-reflex-external-check.md)
(frame-_capture_: a frame is inherited and not re-grounded; the cure is an
external check — precedence-as-approval is the **authority** face of the same
reflex);
[PDR-090](PDR-090-one-law-three-faces.md)
(frame-_slip_: a projection drifts from its source; this PDR is the third
sibling — frame-_deference_: a prior act is mistaken for present authority);
[PDR-084](PDR-084-owner-action-is-not-a-cure.md)
(an owner intervention is evidence to classify, not a cure to copy — the
precedent of how something was once resolved is not approval to resolve it that
way again).

## Context

Decisions, dispositions, and authority leave a trail: a prior agent's
annotation, a status label, a recorded verdict, a "this is how we have always
done it," a who-acted-first ordering. Under time or context pressure the trail
is read as a warrant — _it was decided, so it is approved; it was done this way,
so it is sanctioned; a prior pass marked it, so I may act on the mark._

The trail is **precedence**: evidence that some act occurred before. **Approval**
is something else: a live, locatable authorisation by the party that owns the
decision — the owner for an owner-decision, or the proving surface itself for a
verifiable claim. Precedence and approval coincide often enough that the reflex
to equate them mostly survives — until the case where a precedent was never
actually approved (a prior agent's caution, a stale label, an inherited
convention), and equating them silently re-instates an unauthorised state or
preserves dead weight.

## Decision

**Precedence is not approval. That a thing was decided, annotated, graduated,
labelled, ratified-looking, routed, or done-this-way-before is, by itself, never
authority to keep doing or keeping it. Precedence is only a prompt to re-derive:
locate the authority that actually owns the decision — the owner, or the surface
that would make a claim true — and check it live. Approval must be present and
locatable, never inferred from the existence of a prior act.**

The faces of the one error:

| Precedence (NOT approval)                                   | What approval actually requires                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| a prior agent's annotation ("holding", "ready", "verified") | the owning authority's live decision, or the verification the annotation merely _claims_ |
| a status label (graduated / duplicate / done)               | the proving surface checked now — the home read, confirmed to carry the substance        |
| a recorded verdict or continuity prose                      | the verdict re-grounded as a claim to test, not inherited as the frame                   |
| "it has always been done this way" / an inherited shape     | the shape re-derived from first principles (existence is not correctness)                |
| who acted, broadcast, or arrived first                      | an ordering convention; never authority over the decision itself                         |
| the shape of a prior owner intervention                     | classified as evidence, not copied as a standing cure                                    |

The diagnostic: **whenever "it was already decided / done / marked" is the reason
for an action, that is precedence talking — stop and locate the live approval.**

## Rationale

**Why this needs naming separately from its siblings.** PDR-089 names
frame-capture (do not inherit a frame unre-grounded) and PDR-090 names
frame-slip (do not let a projection drift from its source). Both concern
_epistemic_ inheritance — what is true. Precedence-is-not-approval concerns
_authority_ — what is sanctioned. An agent can correctly re-ground every fact
and still err by treating the precedent of a decision as the decision's
authority. The authority face needs its own name because the tell is different:
not "is this still true?" but "who actually approved this, and is that approval
live?"

**Why it earns its keep (predictive content).** It directs a specific check at a
specific moment: when a prior act is invoked as justification, the cure is to
find the live approving authority or proving surface, never to honour the prior
act as if it carried that authority. It pre-empts two opposite failures at once
— preserving dead weight (keeping something because a precedent kept it) and
re-instating an unauthorised state (acting because a precedent acted).

**Relationship to the verification and tombstone disciplines.** The
verify-don't-trust discipline says a claim is true only when its proving surface
is checked; this PDR is its authority dual — an _authorisation_ is real only
when the approving party is located, never when a prior act stands in for it.
The no-tombstones discipline says a removed idea is stated as present design,
not memorialised; a `graduated` or `duplicate` entry invoked as authority to
delete is precedence, and the home-verification is the authority — which is why
such an entry leaves only after its home is confirmed to carry the substance.

**Alternatives rejected.**

- _Fold into the frame-capture PDR._ Frame-capture is about inheriting a frame's
  content; this is about inheriting a decision's authority. The cures fire at
  different questions; collapsing them loses the authority-specific tell.
- _Leave it as a behavioural note._ The reflex — equate precedence with approval
  — is strong precisely because the two usually coincide; an un-named discipline
  loses to that gravity in exactly the rare case where it matters.

## Consequences

### Required

- When a prior act (annotation, label, verdict, convention, ordering, prior
  intervention) is the reason for keeping or doing something, locate the live
  approving authority or proving surface and check it before acting.
- A disposition that removes or retains on the strength of a status label first
  confirms the underlying authority — the home read and confirmed to carry the
  substance, or the owner's live decision.

### Forbidden

- Treating a prior agent's annotation as the owner's approval.
- Treating "it was done this way" or an inherited shape as authority for doing it
  again.
- Treating temporal precedence (first to act, broadcast, or arrive) as authority
  over a decision.

### Accepted cost

- Re-locating live approval is sometimes redundant in the common case where
  precedence and approval coincide. That redundancy is the price of catching the
  rare case where they diverge — which is exactly the case that silently
  corrupts state.

## Compliance Triggers

- An agent invokes "it was already decided / marked / done this way" as
  justification. Apply the cure: locate live approval.
- A disposition cites a status label or prior annotation as authority. Confirm
  the underlying authority (home / owner) first.
- A second domain surfaces the same shape (a new face). Record it as a row; the
  principle generalises by construction.

## Worked Instances

- A queued item annotated "owner holding" by a prior agent was treated as the
  owner's approval to retain it, when the verification the annotation carried had
  already settled the disposition — precedence (the annotation) stood in for
  approval (the owner's live decision). Re-locating the authority showed the item
  was a confirmed duplicate whose home carried the substance; it was removed.
- A `graduated` or `duplicate` status label is precedence, not authority to
  delete. The authority is the named home read and confirmed to carry the
  substance; only then does the label-bearing entry leave.
